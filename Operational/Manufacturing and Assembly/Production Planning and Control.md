Demand-to-production planning, capacity control and execution governance

# Categorization

Production Planning and Control is correctly categorized under [[Manufacturing and Assembly]]. It translates demand, capacity, material availability and labor availability into an executable production plan.

It should stay close to [[Inventory]], [[Production Scaling]], [[Quality Assurance]] and [[Yield Management]] because production planning decisions directly affect stock levels, capacity bottlenecks, defect risk and usable output.

# Planning stack

- Demand plan: expected sales, orders or replenishment demand.
- S&OP: agreement between sales, operations, finance and supply constraints.
- Master production schedule: what will be produced, when and in what quantity.
- Material requirements planning: required components, lead times and purchase or work orders.
- Shop-floor schedule: machine, labor, batch and sequence plan.

# Decision tools

- Capacity planning: compare required hours against available machine and labor hours.
- Bottleneck analysis: identify the constrained resource limiting throughput.
- Takt time: align production pace with customer demand.
- Little's Law: WIP = throughput * flow time.
- Pareto delay analysis: rank delay causes by lost hours, missed orders or customer impact.
- MRP netting: calculate net material requirement after on-hand stock, open orders and safety stock.

# Metrics

- Plan attainment
- Schedule adherence
- Capacity utilization
- Throughput
- Cycle time
- Queue time
- Changeover time
- WIP level
- On-time production completion
- Material availability
- Labor productivity
- OEE

# Operating questions

- What demand can be fulfilled with current capacity and inventory?
- Which station, supplier or skill is constraining throughput?
- Which orders should be expedited, delayed, split or substituted?
- Which product mix maximizes contribution margin under capacity constraints?
